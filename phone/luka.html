LUKA is a complicated romantic. Balancing a songwriting voice that straddles the starry-eyed idealism of Jonathan Richman and the sardonic devotionals of Leonard Cohen, romance is a fertile but often contradictory subject for Toronto singer-songwriter Luke Kuplowsky. LUKA's rich mesmerizing tenor and nylon-string guitar set the stage for a musical space where his stories and meditations can be rendered with empathy and humour. LUKA's forthcoming album Summon Up a Monkey King is a work of melancholic and joyful reflections on love that recalls the earnest pop sensibilities of Belle and Sebastian and The Magnetic Fields. The album is a work of collaboration with Toronto artists and frequent collaborators Stephen Prickett, Evan Cartwright, Julie Arsenualt and Ada Dahli. Summon Up A Monkey King will be released by Yellow K Records in Spring 2016.
